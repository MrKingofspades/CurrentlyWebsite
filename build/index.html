Maui government lawsuit accuses Hawaiian Electric Industries of causing Lahaina wildfires Maui County alleges that the fires were ignited by "HECO’s energized and downed power lines," causing an estimated $5.5 billion in damages.

1. **Lawsuit Against Hawaiian Electric Industries (HECO)**: The County of Maui has filed a lawsuit against Hawaiian Electric Industries, Inc. (HECO) and its subsidiaries, including Maui Electric Company, Ltd., Hawaiian Electric Company, Inc., and Hawaiʻi Electric Light Company, Inc., for civil damages related to recent wildfires in Lahaina and Kula.

2. **Allegations of Negligence**: The lawsuit claims that HECO acted negligently by not powering down their electrical equipment despite a National Weather Service Red Flag Warning on August 7th. It alleges that HECO's energized and downed power lines ignited dry vegetation, such as grass and brush, leading to the fires.

3. **Extent of Damages**: The fires in Lahaina and Kula burned over 3,000 acres and destroyed more than 2,200 structures. The estimated damage caused by these fires is reported to be around $5.5 billion.

4. **Unaccounted Individuals and Fatalities**: Maui County has released a list of 388 individuals who were reported unaccounted for following the wildfires. Additionally, eight individuals have been confirmed dead due to the fires, contributing to a total of 46 identified victims out of 115 fatalities.

5. **Fire Containment**: The article provides updates on the containment of the fires. As of the article's date, the Olinda fire is 85% contained, the Kula fire is 85% contained, the Lahaina fire is 90% contained, and the Pulehu/Kihei fire was declared 100% contained on August 12th.
